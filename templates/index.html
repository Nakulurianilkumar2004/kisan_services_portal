History of Farming

Farming has been a cornerstone of human civilization for thousands of years. It began during the Neolithic Revolution, approximately 10,000 years ago, when humans transitioned from a nomadic lifestyle of hunting and gathering to settled agriculture. Early farming practices included the cultivation of crops such as wheat, barley, and rice, and the domestication of animals like sheep and cattle.

Ancient civilizations, including Mesopotamia, Egypt, and the Indus Valley, developed advanced techniques such as irrigation and plowing, allowing for large-scale farming and surplus food production. These innovations laid the foundation for the growth of cities and the advancement of human societies.

Over time, traditional farming methods evolved with the introduction of crop rotation, the use of fertilizers, and mechanized tools during the Agricultural Revolution. Today, modern agriculture combines these traditional practices with cutting-edge technology to meet the needs of a growing global population.
